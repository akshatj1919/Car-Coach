# EASY (15)
easy|You are driving past a school during school zone hours. What must you do?|A) Slow down to the posted school zone limit|B) Maintain normal speed if no children are visible|C) Sound your horn to warn children|D) Stop completely until school finishes|A
easy|When you see a yellow traffic light, you should…|A) Speed up to get through before it turns red|B) Prepare to stop if it’s safe to do so|C) Always stop, no matter what|D) Continue through without slowing down|B
easy|When operating at night on a roadway that has streetlights, you should use…|A) High beam headlights at all times|B) Low beam headlights|C) Park lights only|D) Hazard lights|B
easy|At a STOP sign you must…|A) Slow down and continue if clear|B) Come to a complete stop and proceed when safe|C) Stop only if another vehicle is coming|D) Keep moving slowly|B
easy|When you see children near the road, you should…|A) Drive faster to pass them quickly|B) Slow down and be prepared to stop|C) Ignore them if on the footpath|D) Sound the horn|B
easy|What does a flashing red traffic light mean?|A) Stop and give way before proceeding|B) Drive slowly through the intersection|C) Same as a green light|D) Caution only|A
easy|When driving near parked cars, you should…|A) Keep as close as possible to the parked cars|B) Drive in the middle of the road|C) Watch for doors opening and keep a safe distance|D) Speed up to pass quickly|C
easy|Before you change lanes, you must…|A) Check mirrors and blind spot and signal|B) Just signal|C) Just check mirrors|D) Honk to alert other drivers|A
easy|In NSW the default speed limit in built-up areas is…|A) 40 km/h|B) 50 km/h|C) 60 km/h|D) 70 km/h|B
easy|When driving in fog, you should use…|A) High beam headlights|B) Hazard lights|C) Low beam headlights|D) No lights|C
easy|When turning left at a green light, you must…|A) Give way to pedestrians crossing|B) Turn immediately|C) Wait for a green arrow|D) Honk before turning|A
easy|You must not park your vehicle within how many metres of an intersection with traffic lights?|A) 1 metre|B) 3 metres|C) 10 metres|D) 20 metres|C
easy|You are being overtaken by another vehicle. You should…|A) Increase your speed|B) Move left and allow them to pass|C) Sound your horn|D) Move right|B
easy|What does a red traffic light mean?|A) Stop|B) Go if the road is clear|C) Proceed with caution|D) Stop only if there is traffic|A
easy|When approaching a pedestrian crossing, you must…|A) Continue if no one is on the crossing|B) Be ready to stop for pedestrians|C) Stop only if children are present|D) Sound your horn|B

# MEDIUM (15)
medium|When approaching a railway level crossing with flashing red lights, you must…|A) Slow down|B) Stop until the lights stop flashing|C) Proceed carefully|D) Honk before crossing|B
medium|When driving on a freeway, you should…|A) Always drive in the right lane|B) Use the left lane unless overtaking|C) Drive as close as possible to the centre|D) Stop to pick up passengers|B
medium|When joining a motorway, you should…|A) Stop at the end of the merging lane|B) Match your speed to the traffic and merge safely|C) Merge only if there’s a big gap|D) Force your way in|B
medium|When you see a “Give Way” sign, you must…|A) Slow down and give way if necessary|B) Always stop completely|C) Drive through quickly|D) Honk your horn|A
medium|A broken yellow line on the kerb means…|A) Clearway – no stopping during certain times|B) Parking allowed anytime|C) Taxi zone only|D) Bus zone only|A
medium|When you tow a trailer, your maximum speed limit on a highway in NSW is…|A) 100 km/h|B) 90 km/h|C) 110 km/h|D) 120 km/h|B
medium|When driving in the rain, your stopping distance will…|A) Increase|B) Decrease|C) Stay the same|D) Not change if you brake hard|A
medium|You must dip your high-beam headlights when…|A) You see oncoming traffic within 200 m|B) You pass a streetlight|C) You drive on a country road|D) You drive in a tunnel|A
medium|You come to a roundabout and would like to take the right turn. You must…|A) Signal right as you enter and left when exiting|B) Only signal when leaving|C) Signal left throughout|D) Not signal at all|A
medium|If your vehicle begins to skid, you should…|A) Brake hard immediately|B) Steer in the direction of the skid|C) Accelerate|D) Turn sharply the opposite way|B
medium|When you see double unbroken centre lines, you may cross them…|A) To overtake|B) To turn into a driveway|C) Anytime|D) Never|B
medium|When approaching a hill or curve, you should…|A) Drive faster to clear it quickly|B) Stay in your lane and slow down|C) Move to the opposite lane|D) Turn on hazard lights|B
medium|When parking parallel to the kerb, you must leave how much space from the kerb?|A) Within 1 metre|B) Within 50 cm|C) 1 metre or more|D) Any distance|B
medium|When passing a parked bus picking up passengers, you should…|A) Slow down to 40 km/h if within the speed zone|B) Overtake as quickly as possible|C) Sound your horn|D) Ignore and pass normally|A
medium|If you miss your exit on a motorway, you should…|A) Reverse carefully|B) Drive to the next exit|C) Stop on the shoulder and turn|D) Cross the median strip|B

# HARD (15)
hard|You are turning right at an intersection with no signals. Oncoming traffic is going straight. You must…|A) Turn before them|B) Give way to oncoming traffic|C) Honk to alert them|D) Wait in the middle of the road|B
hard|You are at an unmarked T-intersection. Who gives way?|A) The driver on the terminating road|B) The driver on the continuing road|C) The larger vehicle|D) The driver turning right|A
hard|You see a green light but traffic is blocking the intersection ahead. You should…|A) Enter and wait in the middle|B) Wait behind the stop line until clear|C) Drive slowly forward|D) Use the right lane|B
hard|When can you cross a single continuous centre line?|A) To overtake another vehicle|B) To enter or leave a driveway|C) At any time|D) Never|B
hard|A “Shared Zone” speed limit means…|A) 10 km/h maximum and give way to pedestrians|B) 20 km/h and honk before entering|C) 30 km/h with no pedestrians|D) Normal 50 km/h limit applies|A
hard|When approaching a pedestrian with a guide dog, you should…|A) Give way and don’t sound your horn|B) Overtake quickly|C) Honk to warn them|D) Ignore them|A
hard|When approaching a police vehicle with lights flashing, you must…|A) Stop where you are|B) Slow down and move left when safe|C) Speed up to clear the road|D) Continue normally|B
hard|When approaching an emergency vehicle stopped on the roadside with flashing lights, you must…|A) Slow to 40 km/h if safe|B) Continue at the limit|C) Stop immediately|D) Sound horn|A
hard|In heavy rain your car starts to aquaplane. What should you do?|A) Brake hard|B) Ease off accelerator and steer straight|C) Turn the wheel quickly|D) Accelerate to regain traction|B
hard|If you’re involved in a crash, you must…|A) Leave immediately if no one saw you|B) Stop and give your details to others involved|C) Drive to the nearest police station only|D) Keep going if it’s minor|B
hard|You are approaching a “KEEP LEFT UNLESS OVERTAKING” sign you should…|A) Drive in the right lane unless overtaking|B) Stay in the left lane unless overtaking|C) Always drive in the right lane|D) Drive in any lane|B
hard|You see a red “B” light at a traffic signal. It applies to…|A) Buses only|B) Bicycles only|C) All drivers|D) Emergency vehicles|A
hard|When entering a freeway, you must give way to…|A) Vehicles already on the freeway|B) Vehicles behind you|C) Vehicles in the merging lane|D) Trucks only|A
hard|If a vehicle is tailgating you, what do you do?|A) Brake hard to warn them|B) Move left and let them pass safely|C) Speed up|D) Ignore them|B
hard|What is the blood alcohol limit for full licence holders in NSW?|A) 0.00|B) 0.02|C) 0.05|D) 0.08|C
